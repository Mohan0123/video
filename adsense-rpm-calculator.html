What is AdSense RPM?

RPM stands for Revenue Per Mille (Mille is Latin for thousand). It is a metric used by Google AdSense and other ad networks to compare revenue across different channels.

It answers the question: "How much revenue am I generating for every 1,000 views on my page?"

The AdSense RPM Formula

The calculation is straightforward:

RPM = (Estimated Earnings / Page Views) * 1000

For example, if you earned $15 from 5,000 page views:

  • ($15 / 5000) = 0.003
  • 0.003 * 1000 = $3.00 RPM

Benefits of Monitoring RPM

Using a reliable calculator to track RPM helps you:

  • Identify High-Performing Content: See which pages generate the most revenue per view.
  • Optimize Ad Placements: Test different ad locations and see if your RPM increases.
  • Compare Networks: If you use multiple ad networks, RPM gives you a standard metric to compare their performance.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Does a higher RPM mean more traffic?

No. RPM measures efficiency, not traffic volume. You can have low traffic but a high RPM if your ads are very relevant and expensive.

2. How can I increase my AdSense RPM?

To increase RPM, focus on high-paying keywords (niche), improve ad placement (above the fold), and ensure your site loads quickly to view ads.

3. What is the difference between RPM and CPM?

CPM (Cost Per Mille) is what advertisers pay for 1000 impressions. RPM is what the publisher (you) earns per 1000 views. RPM calculates your effective revenue.
