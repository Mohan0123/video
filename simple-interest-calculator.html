How to Use the Simple Interest Calculator

Our Simple Interest Calculator is designed to be intuitive and fast. Whether you are calculating returns on a fixed deposit or estimating interest on a loan, follow these simple steps:

  1. Enter Principal: Input the initial amount of money (P) you are investing or borrowing.
  2. Enter Rate: Input the annual interest rate (R) in percentage. Do not include the % symbol.
  3. Enter Time: Input the duration for the interest calculation.
  4. Select Unit: Choose whether the time entered is in "Years" or "Months".
  5. Calculate: Click the "Calculate Interest" button to see the interest earned and the final total amount instantly.

What is Simple Interest?

Simple interest is a quick method of calculating the interest charge on a loan or the interest earned on a deposit. Unlike compound interest, simple interest is calculated only on the initial principal amount.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Does this calculator work for days?

Currently, this calculator supports Years and Months. To calculate for days, you can convert your days into a fraction of a year (Days / 365) and enter that value in the "Time" field while selecting "Years".

2. What is the difference between Simple and Compound Interest?

Simple interest is calculated on the principal amount only. Compound interest is calculated on the principal amount plus the interest that has accumulated over previous periods. Compound interest grows faster over time.

3. Can I use this for bank loan calculations?

Yes, you can use it for estimates. However, banks often use specific day-count conventions or compound interest methods. This tool provides a mathematical simple interest figure.
